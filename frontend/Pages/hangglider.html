Power Hang Glider
Training

Power hang gliding (or weight-shift control trikes) combines the freedom of a standard hang glider wing with the reliability of a small fuel-efficient engine. It is the perfect entry point for flight enthusiasts.

Our curriculum begins with wing assembly and check routines, progressing through engine maintenance, wind gust vectors, emergency glides, and hands-on flying hours with certified instructors.

Flight Training Syllabus

Syllabus
Phase 1: Rigging & Aerodynamics
  • Wing batten insertions
  • Fabric checks
  • Weight-shift control limits
  • Pre-flight engine spark checks
Phase 2: Runway Takeoffs
  • Throttle control
  • Alignment checks
  • Climb rate angles
  • Wind drift corrections
  • Circuit patterns flying
Phase 3: Cross Country & Glides
  • Engine-out emergency glide profiles
  • Wind thermal maps reading
  • Precision runway landings
